Grouped form fields

This section is NVDA

The information here applies to any set of inputs grouped by a fieldset, such as radios, checkboxes or date components.

A fieldset is what is known as a grouping element and is announced to NVDA to tell the user the inputs inside are related.

It is important that the group is given an accessible name and this function is performed by the legend and is normally the question the group refers to. You'll hear this read out when you reach the group.

You can select a radio or checkbox by pressing enter or space. When you select a radio or checkbox, or move to a selected input, you will hear NVDA announce “checked” (even if it is a radio button).

If you select the input by hitting Enter or space on the label you will enter forms mode. So you will hear a tone to tell you that. Once you are in forms mode, using the up/down arrows will change your selection. To go back to normal navigation, hit the Esc key or the form mode keys Modifier + space.

Where do you live?

When testing, check each group with a fieldset has a legend with copy inside it.
